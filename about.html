About Apex

Denver's roofing company, built on referrals.

For two decades we've climbed Denver roofs, filed insurance claims, and earned the trust of homeowners and business owners up and down the Front Range.

Our story

Apex Roofing was founded in 2005 by a local crew who were tired of seeing Denver homeowners pushed into the wrong roof. We started as a three-person operation working storm repairs in Highlands Ranch and grew the old-fashioned way — one referral at a time.

Today we're an Owens Corning Preferred Contractor and a GAF Certified installer, handling everything from a single missing shingle to full new-construction roof systems. What hasn't changed: every estimate is walked personally, every job gets the crew lead's direct cell number, and no one pays until the final walk-through is signed.

What we stand for

The standards we won't bend on.

These aren't slogans on a truck. They're how every Apex project gets scoped, priced, and installed.

01

Honest scoping

We tell you what your roof actually needs — not what gets us the biggest check. Repair when repair works, replace only when it's time.

02

Our own crews

No subcontracted day labor. Every Apex roof is installed by W-2 crews we've trained ourselves, under the same crew lead from tear-off to cleanup.

03

Insurance fluency

We read policies, document damage the way adjusters need it, and attend the inspection so you aren't negotiating alone on your own driveway.

04

Warrantied for life

Transferable lifetime workmanship warranty on every roof replacement, plus whatever the manufacturer provides on materials. In writing, not in theory.

Certified where it counts.

We're an Owens Corning Preferred Contractor, a GAF Certified installer, and a GoGreen Home Energy Financing certified service provider. Those aren't badges we paid for — they require ongoing training, proof-of-install audits, and customer-satisfaction thresholds.

It means two things for you: we can offer the best manufacturer-backed warranties in the state, and we're held to a higher install standard than a non-certified roofer has to meet.

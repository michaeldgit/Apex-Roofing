Roofs built to shrug off hailstones the size of softballs & wind that rattles the windowpanes.

Twenty-one seasons on the Front Range. 9,400 squares laid. One shop, one crew, one standard — and a ledger full of homeowners who slept through the last storm.

The honest truth of roofing in Colorado is that the sky does most of the work for us. A hailstorm comes through Cherry Hills on a Tuesday afternoon, and by Wednesday morning a dozen roofs are calling for help. We answer the phone ourselves. No sales floor, no commission ladder, no high-pressure knock on the door the week after the clouds clear.

We install Class-4 impact-rated asphalt shingles, standing-seam metal in twelve gauges, TPO and EPDM on the flat work, cedar where the HOA demands it, and synthetic slate where a homeowner wants the look without the eight-hundred-pound weight class. Every deck gets ice-and-water shield two courses up from the eave, stepped flashing on every sidewall, and a six-nail pattern fastened into sheathing we've inspected with our own hands.

If we're not the roofer for your house, we'll say so on the first visit. We'd rather lose the job than lose the reputation. It has taken twenty-one years to build; we have no intention of trading it for a Friday afternoon estimate. If we are your roofer, you will know it within about forty-five minutes. Bring a pot of coffee. Ask every question you can think of. We'll answer honestly, and we'll put it in writing before we leave.

Section III · The Method

Four steps from first ladder to final invoice.

Every job follows the same rhythm. No surprises on day three. No change orders without a signature.

Rooftop Inspection

Our estimator climbs the roof, not the driveway. We chalk every hit, measure every plane, and photograph every penetration. You get the report that afternoon.

Adjuster Meet

We walk the roof with your insurance adjuster and point out what their scope missed. Most of our jobs gain supplemental funds at this stage — legitimately, documented, approved.

Install Week

Tear-off at seven, dry-in by lunch, shingles going down by afternoon. Most residential jobs wrap in two to three days. Driveway swept with a magnet every evening.

Final Walk & Warranty

We walk the property with you, hand over the manufacturer and workmanship warranties in a folder, and file your register card the same day. You have our cell for twenty years.
